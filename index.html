Say Hello to the Fetch API!

This bad boy is your golden ticket to making network requests in JavaScript. Gone are the days of wrestling with XMLHttpRequest—Fetch API to the rescue! Here’s a code snippet with placeholders:

fetch('https://api.openweathermap.org/data/2.5/weather?q={LOCATION}&appid={API_KEY}&units=metric')
  .then(response => response.json())
  .then(data => console.log(data))
  .catch(error => console.error('Oops, something went wrong:', error));

Just replace {LOCATION} with your desired city and {API_KEY} with your personal OpenWeather API key. But wait, you don't have one? No sweat, let's get you sorted out!

Snagging Your OpenWeather API Key

Here's the deal:

  1. First, surf your way to the OpenWeatherMap sign-up page and create an account.
  2. Once you've logged in, navigate to the 'API keys' tab and hit the 'Create Key' button.
  3. Give your key a name that sparks joy, and bam! You've got your very own API key.

Replace that {API_KEY} placeholder with your new key, and you're in business.

Executing Your Script

Ready to run your script? If you're team HTML, just tuck your JavaScript inside a <script> tag at the end of the body section. More of a Node.js wrangler? Crack open your terminal, mosey on over to your script, and command it to life with 'node yourFile.js'. It's as simple as pie!
